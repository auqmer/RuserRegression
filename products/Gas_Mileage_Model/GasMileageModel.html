Predicting Gas Mileage

William Murrah

7/23/2021

The purpose of this report is to generate a predictive model that can be use to budget company expenses for employee gas mileage reimbursement. Data from Henderson & Velleman (1981) were used to build the model, which contained data on 32 automobiles, and originally contained 11 variables. A subset of six variables was selected for model building, which included the outcome variable miles per gallon (mpg) and 5 additional characteristics: number of cylinders (cyl), horse power (hp), weight in 1,000 pounds (wt), engine shape (vs either V-shaped or straight), and transmission type (am, either automatic or manual). Graphical displays of these data are given in Figure 1, while descriptive statistics are in Table 1 and Table 2. Figure 1 is a scatterplot matrix (Revelle, 2021), and indicates that all of the predictors are correlated with the outcome variable.
